Zest UI Redesign

Onboarding UI Redesign

Project Introduction

Below is the result of the collaboration between my team and Zest—an online gifting platform helping brands scale by streamlining the gift-buying experience directly on their website. It offers multi-ship checkout, e-gifting, and corporate storefronts, with a concierge feature for brands to manage or process orders.

Our redesign transforms a fragmented, self-guided setup process into a structured, intuitive flow that reduces reliance on customer support, shortens time to completion, and improves overall usability.

Collaboration Team

Elise Carman Gianna Aiello Karina Valrani Julia Zdzilowska

Considerations & Goals

Zest entrusted us with redesigning their onboarding process for new vendors. A few considerations we took into account when brainstorming collectively the new UI:

  • Reduce reliance on customer support
  • Create a structured, guided experience
  • Maintain flexibility for users with incomplete information
  • Improve task completion rates
  • Enhance overall usability and user satisfaction

Initial Sketches

We started by creating sketches to help visualize our ideas, weigh the benefits of alternative designs, and avoid premature decision-making. We focused on a desktop interface, with my design (sketch 4) focusing on interactivity, wizard guidance (potentially toggled off), and playful visuals.

Key Design Decisions

When designing our solution, we noticed that it is crucial that tasks are skippable, given that clients may not have all the information at the time of onboarding. In order to ensure that clients can be aided in the process of onboarding while still being able to keep track of tasks that they have completed/still need to complete, we made 4 key design choices:

01
Skippable Tasks

Allow users to skip tasks they can't complete immediately

02
Progress Tracking

Clear visual indicators of completed and pending tasks

03
Optional Guidance

Wizard mode for new users, streamlined for experienced ones

04
Contextual Help

"Show hint" option for on-demand assistance

Critiques & Iterations

Having received feedback and clarifications from the company, through discussions with a mentor regarding the lo-fi prototypes, our team reworked our initial proposal. Key changes included:

  • Refined task flow based on user feedback
  • Improved visual hierarchy and clarity
  • Enhanced progress indicators
  • Streamlined navigation between tasks

Final Prototype & Reflections

In our most recent meeting with our mentor, we discussed the importance of designing for the "general user"—someone who may be either highly experienced with similar systems or completely new to onboarding functionalities.

While we've removed the "wizard" option in prior iterations—as it would greatly slow down the former group—we kept the "show hint" option, which Christine appreciated. We've also discussed the necessity of keeping the end-user engaged through the process, and it's certainly an aspect we'd improve on in the future.

Our current impersonal task view could be potentially preceded with a welcome page greeting the user on first run. The transitions between tasks could also be enhanced, possibly with a carousel animation, as pictured in our lo-fi prototypes.

We've also been asked about the progress bar, which we ultimately decided to replace with "estimated completion time" as we were unsure whether the former would relate to all subtasks or just the required ones. In future versions, we'd compare the efficiency of the two, by analyzing their corresponding bounce rates.
